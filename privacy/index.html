7. Google user data; Gmail send connection (OAuth); Limited Use

When a User connects a Gmail account so the Services can send email through that account, SuccessOBM’s Google OAuth client requests exactly two scopes, and no others: https://www.googleapis.com/auth/userinfo.email and https://www.googleapis.com/auth/gmail.send. The same scopes appear together on the Google consent screen for that connection. SuccessOBM does not implement incremental or add-on OAuth scope requests for Google in production.

Not used for platform login. SuccessOBM does not use Google as a sign-in or authentication method for access to app.successobm.com. The Google OAuth flow is used only to authorize a send-mail integration and to obtain the connected account’s email address for display and binding.

No Calendar access. SuccessOBM does not request Google Calendar scopes, does not read or write Calendar data, and does not access Google Calendar APIs.

No mailbox read or modify. SuccessOBM does not request Gmail read or modify scopes. We do not use Google APIs to list, read, search, import, label, archive, or change existing messages in the user’s mailbox. Only sending new messages through Gmail’s send API is authorized.

7.1 Requested OAuth scopes; data accessed; purposes; storage; limitations

OAuth scope What Google permits / what we access Why it is required What SuccessOBM stores
https://www.googleapis.com/auth/userinfo.email The email address of the Google account that authorized the connection (as returned by Google’s userinfo endpoint for the authorized user). To identify and display which Gmail address is linked to the workspace User, to bind OAuth tokens to that integration record, and to distinguish multiple connections where applicable. OAuth tokens as above; send operation logs (including message identifiers or error details returned by Google when applicable); and the message content and recipient data that Users submit for sending, retained as part of normal communications records in the Services.

Reviewer note (scope minimization). The implementation is limited to send plus connected-account email identification. No broader Gmail or Google Workspace data scopes are requested.

7.2 Disconnect, revocation, and token removal

In-app disconnect. When a User disconnects the Gmail integration in app.successobm.com, SuccessOBM removes the connection from the product and deletes or invalidates the stored OAuth credentials (refresh and access tokens and related integration metadata) associated with that link so that the Services no longer send mail through that Google account.

Google-side revocation. Users may also revoke the SuccessOBM application entirely in Google Account settings at https://myaccount.google.com/permissions, which revokes authorization at Google. We recommend using both in-app disconnect (to clear SuccessOBM’s records) and Google revocation if the user wishes to ensure no residual app authorization remains visible in their Google account.

After disconnect or revocation, the Services will not perform further Gmail API calls for that connection.

  • We do not sell Google user data.
  • We do not use Google user data for serving advertisements, except for limited use cases permitted under Google’s policy (for example, as necessary to provide features that include advertising functionality explicitly requested by the user, if ever offered in compliance with Google requirements).
  • We do not use Google user data for purposes unrelated to the Services’ user-facing features without obtaining your prior affirmative agreement.
  • Human access to Google user data is limited: we allow human access only (a) with your consent, (b) for security purposes (for example, investigating abuse), (c) to comply with applicable law, (d) when aggregated and anonymized for internal operations, or (e) to the extent necessary for internal operations and then only by personnel bound by confidentiality obligations and subject to technical and organizational controls.
  • We do not transfer Google user data to third parties except as permitted under the Limited Use requirements (for example, as necessary to provide or improve user-facing features; for security; to comply with law; with your consent; or as part of a merger, acquisition, or sale of assets with notice and safeguards as required).
