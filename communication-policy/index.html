Communication Consent Policy

Effective date: April 26, 2026. Last updated: April 26, 2026. This Policy explains how electronic communications work on SuccessOBM properties—successobm.com, app.successobm.com, and forms.successobm.com—and how recipients provide consent and withdraw it. It is incorporated by reference into the Terms of Service.

This Policy distinguishes between (A) communications SuccessOBM sends about its own products, accounts, and legal notices, and (B) communications a Customer (a SuccessOBM workspace owner or user acting on its behalf) sends to its own audience using the Services. Different laws apply depending on the sender, channel, and purpose.

1. Scope and channels

This Policy covers communications sent by email, SMS, and other electronic channels made available through the Services. It supplements the Privacy Policy and Acceptable Use Policy.

2. SuccessOBM communications (our own notices)

We may send:

  • Transactional/service messages: Account verification, security alerts, password resets, billing receipts, subscription and payment notices, integration errors that affect your account, and other non-promotional notices necessary to provide the Services. These may be sent without marketing consent where permitted by law, and you may not be able to opt out of essential service notices while maintaining an active account.
  • Product and marketing messages: Information about features, events, or offers from SuccessOBM. Where required, we obtain consent before sending promotional SMS or email, and we provide opt-out mechanisms as described below.

3. Customer communications (messages to End Users)

When a Customer uses the Services to send email or SMS to its contacts, subscribers, clients, or other individuals (“End Users”), the Customer is the sender for compliance purposes (subject to carrier and provider rules), except where SuccessOBM sends a communication in SuccessOBM’s own name.

Customer is solely responsible for:

  • Obtaining legally required consents and permissions;
  • Providing accurate sender identification and, for commercial email, required physical address or compliant alternatives under applicable law;
  • Honoring opt-outs, STOP requests for SMS where applicable, and preference centers;
  • Ensuring message content is truthful, lawful, and compliant with carrier and messaging provider policies (including Twilio’s requirements and industry guidelines).

SuccessOBM provides tools and configuration options; it does not provide legal advice. Customer’s obligations are also stated in the Terms of Service.

4. How consent is obtained

4.1 SuccessOBM marketing

We obtain consent through signup flows, preference centers, and clear disclosures at the point of collection (for example, marketing checkboxes where required, double opt-in where we elect to use it, or other compliant methods).

4.2 Customer marketing to End Users

Customer must obtain consent (or another lawful basis, where applicable) using disclosures and mechanisms appropriate to the channel and jurisdiction. For U.S. promotional SMS, express written consent is typically required prior to sending marketing messages. Consent should identify the brand End Users will receive messages from and be clear that they are agreeing to receive recurring automated messages if that is the case.

Hosted forms on forms.successobm.com may collect consent on behalf of Customer. Customer is responsible for the accuracy and sufficiency of consent language on those forms.

5. U.S. A2P 10DLC; campaign registration; who is responsible

For SMS sent to U.S. destinations through carrier networks, application-to-person (A2P) 10DLC rules, toll-free message verification, and related Twilio or carrier requirements may require a registered brand, campaign, use-case attestation, and ongoing accuracy of campaign attributes.

Default allocation. Unless SuccessOBM and Customer have agreed otherwise in a signed order form or other written agreement, Customer is solely responsible for: (a) determining whether its messaging requires 10DLC or related registration; (b) submitting and maintaining truthful brand and campaign registrations; (c) ensuring message content, frequency, and opt-in practices match the registered use case; (d) maintaining records of consent and honoring STOP and privacy commitments tied to those programs; and (e) responding to carrier, Twilio, or regulatory inquiries directed at the message sender. SuccessOBM may route messages using Twilio infrastructure, numbering, or subaccounts as a technical facility; that role does not, by itself, transfer Customer’s legal compliance duties to SuccessOBM.

When SuccessOBM registers. If SuccessOBM expressly contracts to register a campaign or brand on behalf of a Customer, the parties’ written agreement will specify fees, information Customer must supply, and each party’s duties; absent that specificity, Section 5’s default allocation applies. See also Terms of Service Section 10.5.

6. SMS: required disclosures, rates, STOP, and HELP

For SMS programs operated through SuccessOBM’s Twilio-based messaging capabilities, the following applies to promotional or recurring SMS unless a message is strictly transactional and exempt under applicable rules:

  • Message and data rates may apply. Carriers are not liable for delayed or undelivered messages.
  • Message frequency: Frequency varies based on Customer settings and End User interactions.
  • Opt-out: End Users can cancel SMS at any time by texting STOP to the number from which they received the message (or the opt-out path presented in the message or signup flow). After STOP, End Users may receive a one-time confirmation message.
  • Help: End Users can text HELP for assistance or contact the sender’s customer care contact provided in the message or the originating brand’s published contact information.
  • Consent is not a condition of purchase where that statement is required for a promotional SMS program under applicable law and the program is structured accordingly.

Customers must present legally adequate disclosures at the point of opt-in, consistent with the program actually configured in the Services. Customers must honor STOP immediately (subject to reasonable technical propagation delays) and maintain suppression records.

7. Email: commercial messages and identification

For commercial email, applicable law may require accurate header information, truthful subject lines, identification of the message as an advertisement where required, a valid postal address or compliant alternative for the sender, and a clear and conspicuous mechanism to opt out of future marketing email.

When Customer sends commercial email through the Services, Customer is responsible for compliance with the CAN-SPAM Act (and similar laws), including honoring opt-out requests within legally required timeframes (typically no later than ten (10) business days for CAN-SPAM marketing opt-outs unless law requires faster handling).

8. AI-assisted drafting

If Customer uses AI Features to draft SMS or email, Customer remains responsible for final content, disclosures, and consent. AI-generated drafts may be inaccurate or non-compliant; human review is required before sending regulated communications.

9. Opt-out, preferences, and suppression

SuccessOBM marketing: You may unsubscribe from promotional emails using the link in those emails or by contacting privacy@successobm.com. SMS opt-out for SuccessOBM-owned programs uses STOP as described in applicable signup disclosures.

Customer messages: End Users should use the Customer-provided unsubscribe mechanism (email link or SMS STOP). End Users may also contact Customer directly. If an End User contacts SuccessOBM about Customer-sent messages, we may forward the request to the relevant Customer and take reasonable technical steps consistent with law and provider policies.

SuccessOBM may maintain platform-level suppression and anti-abuse controls to prevent messaging to opted-out numbers or addresses associated with a workspace.

10. Recordkeeping

We may retain consent records, message metadata, and configuration logs as needed to demonstrate compliance, resolve disputes, and operate the Services, as described in the Privacy Policy.

11. Third-party carriers and delivery

SMS and email delivery depend on carriers, inbox providers, and intermediaries. SuccessOBM is not responsible for carrier delays, filtering, blocking, or failures outside SuccessOBM’s reasonable control.

12. Changes

We may update this Policy by posting a revised version with a new effective date. For material changes affecting consent practices, we will provide notice as required by law or the Terms of Service.
