The Problem

Most businesses grow faster than their systems do.

As your online business grows, tools multiply, processes become manual, and no one has a clear view of what's happening across marketing, sales, and operations — all at once.

  • Scattered Tools Across Every Department

    Marketing runs on one platform, sales on another, operations somewhere else — and none of them share data, context, or a unified view of what's actually happening.

  • Manual Work That Doesn't Scale

    Follow-ups, task assignments, and process steps all depend on someone remembering to do them. As the business grows, things get dropped and the workload becomes unsustainable.

  • No Unified View of the Business

    Important data is scattered across multiple tools. Without a single view, it's hard to know what's working, what's stalling, and where to focus your attention right now.

  • Processes That Break as You Add People

    What works when it's just you breaks the moment you add staff, contractors, admins, or partners. Without a shared system, growth creates confusion instead of scale.

The Platform

Every area of your business, managed from one platform

Each area of the business gets its own task types, workflows, and agents — and they all share the same contacts, history, and status view. No separate systems, no manual bridging.

  • Marketing

    Turn marketing work into tasks — newsletters, social posts, blog content, follow-up emails, and SMS campaigns. Agents help draft, schedule, and send so your marketing pipeline keeps moving.

  • Sales

    Leads and inquiries become tasks — with quotes, follow-ups, proposals, and status updates handled through structured workflows. Agents send follow-up messages and move deals forward so no opportunity sits idle.

  • Operations

    Inquiries, quotes, scheduling, fulfillment, and invoicing all flow through tasks. Each step is assigned, tracked, and moved forward — with agents handling reminders and updates so nothing stalls between handoffs.

  • Team & Accountability

    Assign tasks to staff by role, control what each person sees, and delegate workflows to admins and partners. As you add people, the right work goes to the right person — with ownership, status, and accountability built into every task.
