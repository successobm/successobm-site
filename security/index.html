Security

Effective date: April 26, 2026. Last updated: April 26, 2026. This page summarizes how SuccessOBM approaches security for the Sites (successobm.com, app.successobm.com, forms.successobm.com) and the Services. It supplements the Privacy Policy and Terms of Service and is not a contractual warranty of a specific control or certification unless expressly stated in a separate agreement.

No system is perfectly secure. We implement reasonable safeguards appropriate to the nature of the Services and the data processed. Customers remain responsible for account hygiene, role configuration, integration credentials, and lawful use, as described in the Terms.

1. Organizational security

We maintain access and authorization policies designed to limit SuccessOBM personnel access to production systems and Customer Content to what is reasonably necessary to operate, secure, and support the Services, subject to the human-access limitations for Google user data stated in the Privacy Policy. Personnel with such access are subject to confidentiality obligations.

2. Infrastructure and hosting

The Services run on cloud infrastructure provided by vetted vendors. We use industry-standard configurations for network isolation, patching, and monitoring appropriate to our environment. Physical and environmental controls are provided by those vendors under their certifications and attestations.

3. Encryption and transmission

We use encryption in transit (for example, TLS) for browser and API connections to the Services in accordance with current industry practice. Sensitive credentials such as OAuth tokens are stored using protections appropriate to their risk (including encryption at rest where applicable).

4. Authentication and access control

Workspace access requires authentication. Customers configure roles and permissions for staff and contractors. We support security features such as session management and account-level controls as made available in-product. Customers must revoke access for former Users and protect passwords, API keys, and integration authorizations.

5. Logging, monitoring, and abuse prevention

We maintain logging and monitoring to detect unauthorized access, abuse, spam, and phishing patterns, including for hosted forms. We may block or throttle activity that presents security or compliance risk. Retention of security logs is described in the Privacy Policy.

6. Vulnerability management and incidents

We assess and remediate security vulnerabilities according to risk. If we become aware of a breach of SuccessOBM-controlled personal data that requires notification under applicable law, we will provide notices consistent with our legal obligations. Customers should report suspected vulnerabilities or incidents to security@successobm.com and abuse to abuse@successobm.com.

7. Subprocessors and integrations

We use subprocessors (for example, hosting, email/SMS delivery, payments, AI inference) that meet contractual security and confidentiality requirements. Customer-configured integrations (Google, Twilio, Stripe, and others) are subject to those providers’ security practices once data leaves the Services pursuant to Customer configuration. See the Privacy Policy for subprocessors and data flows.

8. Customer responsibilities

Security is shared. Customers are responsible for: accurate account information; enabling appropriate roles; removing compromised credentials; complying with the Acceptable Use Policy; and ensuring that data they collect via forms or messaging complies with applicable law.
