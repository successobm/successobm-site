Acceptable Use Policy

Effective date: April 26, 2026. Last updated: April 26, 2026. This Acceptable Use Policy (“AUP”) governs use of successobm.com, app.successobm.com, forms.successobm.com, and the SuccessOBM Services. It is incorporated by reference into the Terms of Service.

Violations may result in removal of content, throttling or blocking of messaging, suspension of integrations, or termination of accounts, in addition to legal remedies described in the Terms.

1. Purpose

This AUP protects the security, integrity, and reputation of the Services; safeguards End Users and third-party ecosystems (including email providers, carriers, Google APIs, and payment networks); and ensures Customers use SuccessOBM lawfully and responsibly.

2. Prohibited content

You may not use the Services to create, store, send, publish, or solicit content that:

  • Violates applicable law or promotes unlawful activity;
  • Infringes intellectual property, privacy, publicity, or other rights;
  • Is fraudulent, deceptive, defamatory, harassing, hateful, or discriminatory;
  • Contains malware, exploits, or instructions to harm systems or persons;
  • Constitutes sexually explicit material involving minors, or exploits or endangers minors in any way;
  • Violates sanctions, export controls, or regulations applicable to your jurisdiction or ours.

3. Prohibited messaging and marketing practices

Without limitation, you may not:

  • Send SMS or commercial email without legally required consent, identification, and opt-out mechanisms;
  • Use purchased, rented, scraped, or generated lists where consent is required and not documented;
  • Ignore STOP, unsubscribe, or other legally effective opt-out requests;
  • Spoof sender identities, forge headers, misrepresent the origin of a message, or send misleading subject lines;
  • Use the Services to bypass provider safeguards, rate limits, or spam filters in a manner that creates abuse risk;
  • Send messages in categories restricted or prohibited by carriers or SMS providers (including certain high-risk “SHAFT”-related content categories where applicable), or otherwise violate Twilio’s messaging policies or applicable carrier guidelines.
  • Send U.S. A2P traffic without required 10DLC or toll-free registration, or with a registered use case that does not match actual message content or consent practices.

See the Communication Consent Policy and Terms of Service (messaging compliance; Section 10.5).

4. Google integrations and Google user data

You may not use Google OAuth connections or Google user data obtained through the Services in violation of the Google API Services User Data Policy, including the Limited Use requirements summarized in our Privacy Policy. Prohibited conduct includes selling Google user data, using it for unauthorized advertising purposes, human access outside permitted exceptions, or transferring Google user data to unauthorized third parties.

5. AI Features misuse

You may not use AI Features to:

  • Generate unlawful, fraudulent, infringing, or harmful content;
  • Impersonate individuals or organizations in a deceptive manner;
  • Circumvent safety or abuse controls, or probe model vulnerabilities to extract training data or system prompts in violation of our Terms or vendor policies;
  • Make fully automated decisions with legal or similarly significant effects on individuals where such automation is prohibited without appropriate safeguards and lawful basis.

You remain responsible for reviewing AI outputs before external distribution, especially for regulated communications.

6. Hosted forms and phishing

You may not use forms.successobm.com or form features to impersonate others, collect credentials through deceptive “lookalike” flows, distribute malware, or conduct phishing. Forms must clearly identify the collecting brand and lawful purpose.

7. Technical abuse and security

You may not probe, scan, or test the vulnerability of the Services without authorization; circumvent access controls; overload systems; access non-public areas; use credentials other than your own (except as authorized workspace administration); or attempt to reverse engineer non-public APIs except as law permits.

8. Monitoring and enforcement

We may use technical and organizational measures to detect abuse, including spam prevention, rate limits, automated scanning for phishing indicators, and manual review where appropriate. We may cooperate with carriers, inbox providers, law enforcement, and regulators when required.

We are not obligated to monitor all Customer Content but may remove or refuse content, suspend messaging, disable integrations, or terminate accounts to comply with law, protect End Users, or meet provider requirements.

9. Reporting abuse

Report suspected violations to abuse@successobm.com. Include relevant message headers, URLs, timestamps, and workspace identifiers if known. For copyright or trademark complaints, include the information required by our process (contact legal@successobm.com).
